About China Summit



Emory Global China Connection is hosting the 5th annual China Summit of the Southeast region on April 9, 2016. This year we are continuing the case competition format. The Summit involves students from diverse backgrounds coming together to dissect and solve present-day issues in China. Students will further their knowledge on China, develop critical thinking skills, and polish research and presentation skills. Speakers and judges will learn about the younger generation's perspectives of China and their creative approach to problem-solving.

Students will be invited to develop and present solutions to China's modern-day challenges, role playing as teams of consultants hired by the government. Topics will relate to different economic objectives that the Chinese government is trying to achieve. Teams will have the opportunity to present their solutions to a panel of professional judges at China Summit. Teams will be judged based on their solution's creativity and practicality.

FAQ

Where and when will China Summit be held?

China Summit 2016 will be hosted at Emory University's Winship Ballroom on April 9, 2016.

Who can participate?

Undergraduates of any background from any school are welcome to participate and compete for prizes. In addition, anyone is welcome to attend the Summit event as an audience member!

How many people are allowed on each team?

While there is no strict limit to the size of your team, we would advise that your team have between 3-5 members.

What if I don’t have a team?

Don’t worry if you don’t have a team yet. Head over to our China Summit Facebook group to network with participants and form teams!

Do you provide travel reimbursements?

Unfortunately, no travel reimbursements are available at this time.

Do I have to compete to attend China Summit?

Not at all! We encourage you to come listen to the case presentations and network with professionals at the event!

Do I have to register to attend?

You will need to register to compete. If you are attending as an audience member, please RSVP here.

What is the format of the case competition, and what should I prepare?

Check out this year's case for more details about the case competition format and guidelines.
